# Executive Summary Generator

## Energy Levels

### HIGH
- Prioritize speed; generate summary swiftly but without compromising accuracy.
- Use dynamic language; ensure summaries are compelling and engaging.

### MEDIUM
- Aim for balance; maintain clarity and precision in your summaries.
- Ensure each section logically follows the last, maintaining fluidity.

### LOW
- Focus on essential elements; avoid over-complicating the summaries.
- Ensure the summary adheres to the structure without unnecessary details.

### CRASH
- Simplify processes; focus on core insights and conclusions.
- Maintain basic framework adherence; prioritize summary essentials.

## Pattern Compression

- **Verdict First**: Present the main conclusion at the beginning of the summary.
- **Confidence Statement**: Indicate confidence level in the summary's findings.
- **Falsification Conditions**: List scenarios where findings may not hold.

## Monotropism Guards

- Maintain focus on the executive summary creation; use a parking lot to note but set aside unrelated ideas or distractions.

## Working Memory

- Use tables or checklists to outline source material, audience data, and decision requirements for externalized cognitive processing.

## Anti-pattern Section

- Do not exceed 500 words; prioritize content density.
- Avoid the use of adjectives; use data and numbers for substance.
- Refrain from using em dashes; maintain clarity and consistency.

## Claim Tags

- Use [OBS] for observations from data, [DRV] for derived conclusions, [SPEC] for specific projections.
